Buisness Plan

1. Find a location with a high number of passersby. Choices include shopping malls and strip malls near colleges or business centers. Look for features like room in front of the store to place sidewalk tables, enough floor space for tables and chairs, cozy armchairs, sofas and good traffic flow to order, pick up coffee and leave the store. 2. Obtain licenses and permits to comply with restaurant regulations. Contact the county's board of health to find out how to obtain a food handling permit. The typical U.S. process to obtain a food handling permit includes passing an inspection, filling out an application and submitting a fee. Collect state sales tax on items you sell, so obtain a general sales license that allows you to collect these fees if needed. 3. Choose a name and create a logo for the coffee shop something interesting that people will remember. Once you have a name, contact a graphic designer in your area. The graphic designer pairs your store name with a graphic, such as a steaming cup of coffee. Use the logo on business cards, take-out cups and the sign that goes over the front door.
